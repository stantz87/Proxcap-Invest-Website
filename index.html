Why Social Housing

Tangible assets with contracted income.

Social housing investment offers a rare combination of social purpose and financial resilience. With chronic undersupply pushing rents and house prices ever higher, demand for affordable, secure homes continues to outstrip what the market is delivering, creating long-term, stable demand that isn't dependent on economic cycles.

Income is typically underpinned by long leases with housing associations or local authorities, often linked to inflation, giving investors predictable, index-linked returns alongside government-backed counterparties. At the same time, capital is directed toward addressing one of society's most pressing challenges: ensuring people have access to good-quality, affordable homes.

For investors looking to combine dependable income, low correlation to traditional asset classes, and genuine positive impact, social housing stands out as a compelling and forward-looking opportunity.
